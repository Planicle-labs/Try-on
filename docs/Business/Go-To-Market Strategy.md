# Go-To-Market Strategy

## Initial customer profile

Target merchants should look like this:

- India-based Shopify fashion brand.
- Monthly GMV between INR 10L and 1Cr.
- 25 percent or higher return rate pressure.
- Heavy mobile traffic.
- Small internal team that values self-serve setup.

Avoid using complex drape-heavy categories such as sarees as the primary launch wedge.

## Launch thesis

The fastest path is not enterprise sales. It is a product-led Shopify motion with sharp category focus.

## Phase 1: Narrow launch

- Focus on fitted tops, dresses, co-ords, shirts, and straightforward bottoms.
- Sell return reduction and purchase confidence, not novelty.
- Make install-to-live time a core adoption metric.
- Offer a clean try-on button with guided selfie capture and strict image-quality prompts.

## Phase 2: Merchant retention layer

- Add merchant analytics tied to try-on usage and downstream purchase behavior.
- Add email capture inside the try-on flow.
- Add clearer sizing assistance where garment data quality is sufficient.

## Phase 3: Expansion

- Expand category support into more complex fits and Indian fashion subcategories.
- Introduce Merchant Studio workflows for marketing image generation.
- Use the installed base for cross-sell into other Shopify apps from the portfolio.

## Core channels

### Shopify App Store

- Primary acquisition channel.
- Product page must emphasize setup speed, privacy, and quantified merchant pain.

### Founder-led outbound

- Target Indian D2C brands with known return pressure.
- Use concise ROI-based outreach, not generic AI messaging.

### Community distribution

- D2C founder groups, operator communities, and WhatsApp networks are high-leverage referral paths.
- Early merchant wins should convert into testimonial-driven installs.

## Messaging priorities

- Reduce return-related losses.
- Increase shopper confidence.
- Launch in minutes, not weeks.
- No enterprise contract or technical integration burden.
- Privacy-safe image handling.

## Pricing alignment

The competitive range already exists in-market. A three-tier structure remains defensible:

- Entry tier around $20 for low-volume testing.
- Growth tier around $50 for meaningful usage.
- Higher tier near $300 for larger brands and premium support.

Pricing should align with generation limits and merchant ROI visibility rather than feature sprawl alone.

## Success metrics

- Install to first live try-on time.
- Install to first shopper usage.
- Percentage of merchants active after 30 days.
- Cost per generation and gross margin by tier.
- Try-on-assisted conversion correlation.
- Merchant-reported return reduction signals.

## Launch risks

- Poor input photos lowering trust in output quality.
- Sizing claims outrunning validation quality.
- Weak onboarding causing churn before first value.
- Broad category coverage before model performance is reliable.
