# Product Strategy & Risks

## Product thesis

The strongest product thesis in this market is the combination of visual confidence and fit confidence. Sizing-only tools solve half the problem. Try-on-only tools also solve half the problem. The product should unify both, but only where the output quality can be defended.

## Product priorities

### v1 priorities

- Reliable try-on for a narrow set of apparel categories.
- No-code Shopify installation.
- Strong consumer image guidance.
- Privacy-safe transient image processing.
- Merchant-side reporting that proves usage and value.

### v2 priorities

- Better sizing logic for supported garments.
- ROI dashboard for merchants.
- Email capture and remarketing utility.
- More controlled category expansion based on quality benchmarks.

### v3 priorities

- India-specific garment support.
- Stronger model/data loop around South Asian body types and skin tones.
- Merchant Studio use cases for catalog and campaign content.

## Known market lessons

### Distribution beats technical purity

Strong technology without self-serve distribution does not produce a scalable SMB business.

### Long onboarding kills adoption

If merchants do not reach first value quickly, they churn before product quality matters.

### AR is the wrong technical path for apparel

Generative approaches are better suited to soft goods because fabric realism matters more than live overlay.

### Input quality is a product dependency

Bad garment photos and poor selfies directly degrade trust in the output.

### India quality gaps are real

Western-biased training data can create systematic failures for South Asian users. This is not a small edge case for the target market.

## Major product risks

### Sizing overclaim risk

- Incorrect size recommendations damage merchant trust faster than mediocre try-on visuals.
- Any sizing layer needs controlled rollout and explicit scope boundaries.

### Output-quality risk

- Poor generations can harm merchant brand trust.
- Narrow category support is better than broad but unreliable support.

### Privacy risk

- User image handling is highly sensitive.
- The product should process and delete images quickly with auditable deletion behavior.

### Attribution risk

- Merchants want ROI proof, but overstating causal impact creates credibility problems.
- Use conservative language like assisted conversion or conversion correlation until the measurement model is robust.

## Operating principles

- Ship to a narrow wedge first.
- Measure real output quality before expanding category claims.
- Treat onboarding as part of the core product.
- Treat privacy as a trust feature, not just compliance.
- Build the India-specific moat deliberately through testing and data collection.
