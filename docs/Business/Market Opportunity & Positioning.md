# Market Opportunity & Positioning

## Market opportunity

Virtual try-on is a large, fast-growing category with a clear apparel use case. The strongest business case is not novelty. It is return reduction plus conversion lift.

- Global VTON market: $10.9B in 2024, projected to $108.5B by 2034.
- Apparel is the largest category within VTON activity.
- Online fashion returns remain structurally high.
- India is the highest-growth geography in fashion AR and VTON adoption.

## Why this matters for our business

Indian D2C fashion brands face a concentrated pain profile:

- 28 to 35 percent return rates in fashion.
- Reverse logistics costs of roughly INR 150 to 300 per return.
- Mobile-first traffic and shopper expectations.
- Weak product imagery and inconsistent sizing communication.

This creates a strong wedge for a Shopify-native app that helps merchants answer both questions shoppers care about:

- Will this look good on me?
- Will this fit me?

## The core positioning

We are not just another try-on widget. The product position should be:

**Photorealistic virtual try-on plus AI-assisted sizing for Shopify fashion brands, built for Indian and other emerging-market D2C merchants.**

## Structural market gaps

### 1. Accessible 2-in-1 product

Enterprise players prove the value of combining try-on and sizing, but they are not accessible to SMB merchants.

### 2. India-specific readiness

The market is largely Western in training data, garment assumptions, and go-to-market focus. This leaves Indian body types, skin tones, and garment categories underserved.

### 3. Merchant ROI visibility

Competitors sell with generic uplift claims. Merchants want app-specific evidence tied to their store.

### 4. Self-serve onboarding

The market still leans on demos, services, and lengthy setup. Fast time-to-value is a durable differentiator.

## Strategic positioning statement

We should position the product as:

**The first self-serve Shopify app that combines realistic try-on and sizing intelligence for SMB fashion brands, with a roadmap intentionally tuned for Indian commerce realities.**

## Differentiators to emphasize

- Sizing plus visual confidence in one flow.
- Self-serve onboarding instead of enterprise onboarding.
- SMB-friendly subscription pricing.
- Merchant-facing ROI and analytics.
- India-aware testing, model evaluation, and eventually garment/category support.

## Wedge market

The best initial segment is:

- Indian Shopify D2C apparel brands.
- Monthly GMV roughly INR 10L to 1Cr.
- Return-heavy categories like fitted western wear, ethnic fusion, and everyday apparel.
- Mobile-led traffic and founder-led operations.

## What not to claim too early

- Universal support for complex draped garments.
- Perfect sizing accuracy before validation data exists.
- Causal revenue attribution from try-on events alone.

## Long-term moat

The moat is not the base model. It is the operating layer we build around it:

- Merchant distribution through Shopify.
- India-specific quality and fit data.
- Garment-level sizing knowledge over time.
- Merchant trust from transparent privacy and measurable value.
