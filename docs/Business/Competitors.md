# Competitor Landscape

## Core takeaway

The market is fragmented across Shopify-native apps, API/model providers, and enterprise platforms. No player cleanly combines photorealistic virtual try-on, sizing intelligence, self-serve Shopify onboarding, and India-specific readiness at SMB price points.

## Direct competitors

### GenLook

- Market leader among generative try-on Shopify apps.
- Strongest advantage is first-mover distribution and simple no-code install.
- Clear weakness is the lack of sizing intelligence.
- India is already a meaningful share of installs, which validates demand in our target geography.

### Antla

- Emerging Shopify-native try-on app with SMB-friendly pricing.
- Strong on realism and straightforward packaging.
- Weak on sizing intelligence, analytics depth, and India-specific positioning.

### Looksy

- Competes on low-friction consumer UX.
- Fast browser-based flow is attractive for shopper adoption.
- Merchant value layer is weak because email capture and analytics appear limited.

### PICTOFiT

- Strong product depth with avatar-based try-on, size visualization, and outfit mixing.
- Weak fit for our segment because pricing and setup overhead are too high for SMBs.
- Relevant as a product benchmark, not as the practical default choice for Indian D2C brands.

## Indirect competitors

### 3DLOOK / YourFit

- Best example of a true sizing-plus-visual try-on product.
- Strong technical benchmark with documented return reduction impact.
- Not self-serve and not SMB-accessible, which creates the gap we can occupy.

### True Fit

- Dominant sizing-intelligence platform with a major data moat.
- Solves fit confidence but not visual confidence.
- Strong competitor for the sizing narrative, not for the full product experience.

### Virtusize

- Mature sizing comparison product with global brand relationships.
- Useful proof that sizing remains a durable pain point.
- Limited threat because it does not solve visual try-on.

### Vue.ai

- Enterprise-focused platform with VTON as one module inside a broader suite.
- India-headquartered but not meaningfully positioned for Indian SMB fashion brands.

### Veesual

- Strong enterprise visualization and mix-and-match experience.
- Weak fit for our segment because it is not self-serve and does not offer sizing intelligence.

## Infrastructure providers, not primary competitors

### Fashn.ai

- Best near-term infrastructure option for production-grade VTON speed and quality.
- Threat increases only if it builds a Shopify-native merchant product.

### fal.ai

- Useful for prototyping and model comparison.
- Less reliable as a full production backbone for a merchant-facing product.

### Replicate

- Good for experimentation and low-volume testing.
- Cold starts and inconsistent deployment quality limit production suitability.

## Competitive gaps we can own

- Affordable sizing plus visual try-on in one Shopify app.
- Fast self-serve onboarding with no enterprise sales motion.
- Merchant ROI visibility instead of generic case-study claims.
- India-specific testing, positioning, and eventually model/data tuning.
- A merchant utility layer that combines conversion tooling, lead capture, and content generation.

## What to monitor

- Whether GenLook adds sizing recommendations.
- Whether Antla reviews expose repeated merchant complaints we can position against.
- Whether Fashn.ai launches a Shopify distribution layer.
- Whether any competitor starts explicitly targeting Indian D2C apparel brands.
